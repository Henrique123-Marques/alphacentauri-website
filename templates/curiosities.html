Curiosities

-The star Alpha Centauri C is also known by astronomy as Proxima Centauri, for being the second closest star after the Sun, but it is not visible to the naked eye, unlike Alpha Centauri A and B;

- A project called StarShot, of which the late Stephen Hawking was a part, has a plan to launch a fleet of solar sails to this star system at 20% of the speed of light, with the possibility of getting there within a margin of up to 20 years;

- The movie Avatar, by director James Cameron, takes place in this star system, but Pandora (the moon where the story takes place) is fictional.

Orbit of system

Image showing in the center the stars Alpha Centauri A and B orbiting a common center of mass and outside the star Proxima Centauri (Alpha Centauri C) orbiting them.

- The star Proxima Centauri (Alpha Centauri C) takes about 550,000 years to orbit Alpha Centauri A and B;

- Among the three stars in the Alpha Centauri system, Proxima Centauri is the smallest in mass and the least bright;

- Light from the Alpha Centauri system takes a little over 4 years to be seen in one night here on Earth.
